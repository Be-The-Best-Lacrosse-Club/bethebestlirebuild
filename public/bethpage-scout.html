Are they running man with adjacent slide, crease slide, or a zone? Where do slides come from? How quickly? Gorman is 4th year — expect discipline.

4. Ride Package

Do they run a 10-man? Standard zone ride? Keep a safety? South Side has an elite middie in JJ Hawthorne — how did Bethpage handle him up top? Watch their LSM matchup.

Late-quarter possessions and extra-man sets reveal their money plays. Write down their EMO formation (1-3-2? 3-3? 1-4-1?) and any repeated cuts.

Bethpage Offense — Film Breakdown

How They Attack

Core Problem

Bethpage averages 8.3 goals/game. In their one tough matchup this year (April 8 loss), they still only scored 7. Against Long Island Lutheran (weak), only 7. Their ceiling is 8 goals against a real defense. If we hold them there, we win.

Who Has the Ball

Primary Dodger: #27 Charlie Abramo (A/M, Jr)

Listed first on the roster — that's not an accident. With Reinhardt (67 pts) graduated, Abramo is the primary offensive initiator. Expect him to touch the ball on 40%+ of their offensive possessions.

Watch his hand: A/M hybrid means he can roll between midfield and attack. If he's positioned at the top of the offense as a middie, he's likely a dodger from up top. If at X or the wing, he's a feeder/finisher.

Tendency prediction: Junior A/M types typically dodge to their strong hand from up top, split-dodge at GLE, and look for the crease feed if they don't get topside.

Secondary Threat: #19 Luca Cataldo (A/M, Jr)

Lacrosse family — Leo's younger brother. Moved from FO/M last year to A/M this year. That position change is telling: they need his scoring, not his faceoffs.

Bloodline players tend to have advanced IQ. Expect clean footwork, good off-ball movement, and unselfish decision-making. He'll find the open man.

Bethpage likely runs 2-man action between Abramo (#27) and Cataldo (#19) — their two best offensive weapons. Watch for picks between them up top.

Inside Finishers

#11 Adam Penge (A, Jr) and #33 Alex Nicki (A, Soph) are their pure attackmen. Expect them on the crease and at X, not dodging.

Penge as a junior attackman with no A/M designation = likely a crease finisher or off-ball cutter. Feed target. Our crease defenseman cannot leave him.

Nicki as a sophomore A = young but on the field. Probably takes smart, limited touches. Shut off first, recover to your man.

Projected Offensive Set

Most Likely: 2-2-2 or 1-4-1

Based on the personnel distribution (2 pure A, 3 A/M hybrids, 1 clear #1 midfielder group), Bethpage likely runs:

  • 2-2-2 — Abramo and a middie up top, two wings, Penge and Nicki at X and crease. Balanced. Good for 2-man games.
  • 1-4-1 — If they want isolation, they'll spread 4 across. Abramo or Cataldo dodges from up top while the 4 wings hold their defenders.

From the Southside film, note: Do they run the same set every time, or do they flex? A 4th-year coach under Gorman will have at least 2 sets dialed in.

EMO (Extra Man Offense)

Expected Set: 1-3-2 or 3-3

Bethpage has the shooters to run 1-3-2 (Abramo point, two on wings, two at X). If Gorman is old-school, expect 3-3.

Their EMO is probably average. 4-3 record + low goal volume suggests they don't have a dominant EMO. Aim for 25-35% conversion.

Our EMD job: Tight slide package. Don't give up skip passes. Force low-percentage shots from the wings.

Transition Offense

Their Weakness — Limited Breaks

Without Leo Cataldo's 68% faceoff dominance, Bethpage cannot reliably create transition offense. Their 3 senior FOGOs are an untested committee.

Expect their middies to push hard after saves (not off faceoffs). Watch Chase Solliday #9, Michael Gaudio #26, Liam Farrell #3 — seniors who will run the field.

Our defensive transition: Get bodies back immediately. No ball-watching. If we win the X and push, we score. If they win the X and push, we gave them the one area they can score quickly.

Clearing

Standard Clears, Pressure With Care

Gorman is 4th year and the team has senior D. Expect clean, standard clearing — won't beat themselves.

Their goalie outlets will likely go to a middie or defenseman in the box. Watch their clear pattern on the film — do they go same side, opposite, or over the top?

Our ride: If we're up by 2+, standard 3-3-1. If we need the ball back, escalate to a 10-man or partial 10-man. They have enough senior poise to not panic but untested FOGO means untested clear decisions on faceoff wins.

The Key Question

Identify on Film

Bethpage needs someone to replace 67 points of scoring. That's 2-3 goals per game. Is there ONE player doing it, or is it by committee?

If one guy: Pole him. Shut him down. We win.

If committee: They won't generate enough shots. Play sound team D, we win.

Either way — count how many goals come from Abramo #27, Cataldo #19, Penge #11, and Nicki #33 in the Southside game. That distribution tells you their identity.

Bethpage Defense — Film Breakdown

How They Stop You

Core Truth

Bethpage gave up 13 goals on April 8. That's not a good defense getting unlucky — that's a defense that breaks down when pressured. Apply the pressure. Push transition. Force their slides. The cracks are there.

Personnel Read

Senior-Heavy Pole Group

#18 Luke Waxer (D/LSM, Sr) — their #1 pole. Matches our best middie. 4-year varsity guy = expect technique, footwork, stick on stick.

#42 Vinny Morice (D, Sr) — senior close D. Likely their communicator. Probably takes our X attackman.

#15 Tristan Maricondo and #33 Brady McElwee (D/LSM, Jrs) — the junior depth. They're solid but this is where we attack.

The Vulnerability — Young Back End

#20 Finn Castle (D, Soph) — sophomore close D. If he's starting, he's the weakest link in the starting 7.

#14 Anthony Rapacki (D/LSM, Freshman) — 9th grader at D/LSM. If he sees field time, attack him immediately. Dodge right at him.

#21 Brody Jackson (LSM, Soph) — if he's the starting LSM, we have a matchup. Put our best dodging middie right on him.

Projected Defensive Scheme

Most Likely: Man-to-Man with Adjacent Slide

Gorman in year 4 + senior leadership = disciplined man-to-man. No surprise zone looks. Standard slide package.

Adjacent slide is the default. First help comes from the nearest defender to the ball. Creates a moment of vulnerability for the pass man.

What to exploit: When they slide adjacent, the slid-FROM defender recovers. Skip-pass to the far wing is open for 1-2 seconds.

Possible: Zone on Certain Looks

Senior-heavy teams sometimes flip to zone (usually backer or 3-3) in late-game situations or against dominant dodgers. Watch the Southside film for zone possessions — did they switch schemes after Southside scored 2-3 in a row?

If they zone: attack with ball movement + skip passes. Don't dodge into it. Move the zone until you find the soft spot.

Goalie Scouting

Who's In the Cage?

Bethpage has 3 goalies listed. Starter is one of two scenarios:

Scenario A — #44 Leo Augugliaro (Soph): Young. Nerves a factor. Shoot high early, hot shots, test his reaction time. Look for him to oversell — fake low, shoot high.

Scenario B — #28 Joe DiNuzzo (Sr): Senior experience. Picks his spots, squares up well. Work for quality looks. Don't force bad shots. He'll save bad shots. Good shots at the corners go in.

Note: #25 Joey Traversa (Sr) is option 3. Likely a backup.

Ride Package

Expected: Standard Zone Ride

Bethpage isn't a known 10-man team. Expect standard 3-3-1 zone ride — three pressure the ball, three at midline, one safety back.

Their 3 attackmen don't have Elite-level ride chops (no indication of hard-riding reputation). They'll slow us down but not trap us.

BUT: Don't rule out a 10-man or partial 10-man late in the game if they're down. Gorman might pull it out as a changeup. → See the 10-Man Ride playbook in the nav.

Man-Down Defense (EMD)

Expected: Box and One or Rotating Box

Standard man-down. Focus on collapse and communication. They'll likely force us to shoot from the wings.

Exploit: Skip passes diagonally. Break their box geometry. Wait for the rotation to open the crease.

Their man-down is likely the best part of their defense — senior-heavy team, practiced discipline, clear roles. Pick our moments.

The Critical Read

Plainedge Attack Plan

Their defense is solid against the 1st shot. Bethpage gets beat on 2nd-shot attempts. Rebound opportunities, groundball battles after shots, and second-possession offense.

If we shoot, everyone crashes. The loose ball in front of their cage is where they break down.

Count on the Southside film: how many of Southside's goals came from 1st-shots vs 2nd-shots? That tells us where the crack is.

Tactical Read

Strengths & Weaknesses

Weaknesses — Attack These

1. Face-Off X

Lost Cataldo's 68% win rate. New FOGO has 4-7 games of varsity experience. Their offense can't score if they don't have the ball.

Action: Our best FOGO every draw. Aggressive wings. Count on 55%+ possession advantage.

2. Limited Offensive Firepower

7 goals vs LI Lutheran, 7 goals when they lost by 6. They're averaging 8.3/game — that includes scoreline-inflating wins. Against real defenses, they top out around 7-8.

Action: Keep it tight. If we're within 2 in the 4th quarter, we win — they don't have a go-to goal scorer.

3. Defense Under Pressure

April 8 loss: gave up 13 goals. A well-coached defense doesn't give up 13 unless someone is beating them consistently off the dodge or in transition.

Action: Push transition. Early offense before they set. If they are in their 6v6, dodge hard — make their sliders work.

Strengths — Respect These

1. Coaching Experience

Gorman in year 4 = no mental mistakes, no dumb penalties. They will be in the right spots at the right time.

Action: Don't count on easy looks. Earn every shot. Work through our progressions.

2. Senior Leadership

LILJ explicitly called out their experience and leadership. Translates to composure — they won't melt in big moments.

Action: Start fast. Build a lead early so they have to come out of their comfort zone to chase.

3. Home/Crowd Factor

Bethpage community is tight-knit — expect a real atmosphere if it's at their place.

Action: Score first. Silence the crowd. Don't let the game get emotional.

Plainedge Game Plan

How We Win

  1. Win the X — Own Possession

    Our starting FOGO every draw. Wings pressure their FOGO's exit. Target: 60% faceoff win rate. Every extra possession is 30+ seconds they don't have the ball.

  2. Push Transition Every Time

    On groundballs, faceoff wins, and saves — RUN. Their defense is disciplined but hasn't shown elite recovery. Numbers game: 4v3s and 5v4s are free goals.

  3. Pole Their #1 Dodger

    Once we identify from the Southside film who is replacing Reinhardt, our best LSM takes him every possession. Make him beat a long stick.

  4. Work the 2-Man From X

    Their defense relies on slides. Run our X 2-man to force their slide package. If they adjacent-slide, skip to the open. If they crease-slide, feed the crease.

  5. Don't Force Shots

    If it's not there, work the ball. They don't have the offense to beat us in a shootout — we don't need to beat them in one either. Possession first, shots second.

  6. Clear Clean

    Unknown if they ride hard. Expect standard 6-man zone. Adjacent pass, over the top when pressured. No risky middle passes in our own zone.

  7. Start Fast, Silence the Crowd

    First goal matters. If we score in the first 2 minutes, their senior leadership has to lean on experience instead of momentum. Take that away from them early.

Winning Score Target

Hold them to 7 goals. Score 10+. Their 2026 ceiling on offense is 8. Our job: be the real defense they struggled with on April 8.
