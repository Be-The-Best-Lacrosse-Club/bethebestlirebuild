Lynbrook Offense

How They Attack

Core Weakness

Lynbrook averages 8.7 GF/game but gives up 9.0 — they can score but not enough to win shootouts. Last year they averaged ~10.4 GF with more seniors; they've dropped almost 2 goals/game offensively.

Primary Attack Group

Kaden Rogers + Brandon Scala — The Senior Duo

Two senior attackmen carrying the offense. Rogers is the captain — he's the initiator, takes the pressure touches.

Tendency read: Senior attackmen tend to dodge from X if they're feeders, or from up top / wing if they're shooters. The Rogers/Scala tandem is probably a 2-man game at GLE.

Our counter: Lock off one of them (force the other to create). Put our #1 pole on Rogers.

Patrick Algozzini — The Junior Glue

Junior attackman. Less of a star but likely the smart off-ball mover. Cuts through the middle, back-pipes off Rogers drives.

Our crease defender cannot leave him. If we're watching Rogers, Algozzini will back-cut for goals.

Projected Offensive Set

Likely: 2-3-1 or 1-3-2

With 2 senior attackmen + 1 junior, a point-based set makes sense:

  • 2-3-1 — Two middies up top (Grimes + Mignella), three across (Rogers + Algozzini + Scala), one at X. Balanced with their seniors distributed.
  • 1-3-2 — Classic motion set. One up top, three across, two at X. Good for 2-man at X with their senior attackmen.

Transition

Their Only Big Play Threat

With a less potent half-field offense, transition is where Lynbrook can steal goals. Watch for:

  • Fast breaks after saves (their goalie Lunati is new — but if he makes a save, they'll push)
  • Thomas Dunne winning draws clean → direct to offense
  • Senior middies pushing numbers off turnovers

Our defensive transition: Sprint back, no ball-watching. They don't have elite skill players — they need the numbers advantage to score.

Lynbrook Defense

How They Stop You

Giving Up 9 Goals/Game

Lynbrook's D is the weak point. 63 goals allowed in 7 games = they're giving up 9.0 per game. Coach Luzzi himself said the team needs to "tighten up defensively."

If we put up 10, we win.

Personnel Read

Senior Leadership (Dellacona, Potash)

Two senior close defenders. Experience + discipline. They'll communicate, slide well, play sound team defense.

But senior D-men don't have the athleticism of college-track players. They'll struggle with speed.

Young Core (Pisano, Chatterton — Sophomores)

Two sophomore D-men in the rotation. Pisano called a "workhorse" = high motor. Chatterton is a "newcomer" — brand new to varsity.

Target Chatterton first. Attack him immediately — his first possessions will be the shakiest.

Projected Defensive Scheme

Most Likely: Man-to-Man with Adjacent Slide

Same coach as last year = same system. Last year's team ran straight man with standard slides. Expect the same.

Last year's film is relevant. Pull Hudl tape from our 12-11 playoff win — their scheme is the same, just younger personnel.

Possible: Zone When Tired

Rebuilding teams often resort to zone to hide weaknesses. If they get worn down or we hit them with dodging early, they might flip to a 3-3 or backer zone.

Against zone: Move the ball, don't dodge into it. Skip passes find the seams.

Goalie — The Biggest Hole

Shoot Early, Shoot Often

Ethan Lunati is a first-year starter. Coach Luzzi's #1 preseason concern. Junior goalie with no varsity starting experience = nerves, positioning mistakes, slow reaction time.

Our approach:

  • Shoot on net from the first possession — no fancy plays early
  • Test all angles — high, low, corners
  • Crash the cage on every shot (he won't hold rebounds well)
  • If we score 2 fast, he'll deflate — pour it on

Ride

Expected: Standard Zone Ride

Class C team, rebuilding. Unlikely they run a sophisticated 10-man ride. Expect basic 3-3-1 or 3-4 zone ride.

Clearing should be straightforward. Standard clears, no need for our 10-man ride counters.

Man-Down Defense (EMD)

Unknown — Watch Film

No specific intel on their EMD. Standard approach: expect a box with rotation. They've given up 9 goals/game in even strength — when man down, they'll give up even more.

Take smart penalties. If we draw penalties, our EMO should convert at 35%+ against this defense.

Plainedge Game Plan

How We Win

  1. Start Fast — Shake the New Goalie

    First 3 minutes: shoot at Lunati. Make him make saves. Score early, rattle his confidence. A shaky goalie on a rebuilding team = snowball effect.

  2. Win the X Against Dunne

    Thomas Dunne is a senior FOGO but not dominant. Our #1 FOGO should win 55%+. Every extra possession is a goal they can't generate.

  3. Pole Rogers, Shut Off Scala

    Their two senior attackmen carry the offense. Pole Rogers, put pressure on Scala to create. Force Algozzini (junior) to beat us.

  4. Target the Sophomore Defenders

    Pisano (soph) and Chatterton (soph) are young. Dodge right at them. Force their sliders to commit. If Chatterton is on the field, he's our primary target — new to varsity.

  5. Hunt the Freshman Middies

    Ceglie and Marinos are 9th graders. Any time one of them is matched against our offensive middie, dodge immediately. No hesitation. Young freshmen at the D1 game speed = mistakes.

  6. Control Transition

    Their only big-play threat is transition. Sprint back. No ball-watching. No turnovers in our offensive half — that's where they score.

  7. 10 Goals Is the Target

    They give up 9.0/game. Put up 10+, game is over. Don't settle for bad shots — we have the talent to generate clean looks.

  8. Don't Get Too Comfortable

    Luzzi coaches them tight. They WILL play 4 quarters. They played us to 1 goal last year. Lead 4+ in the 4th quarter or nothing is safe.

Winning Score Target

Us 12, Them 7. Their ceiling is 8-9 goals. Ours should be 12+ against this defense.
