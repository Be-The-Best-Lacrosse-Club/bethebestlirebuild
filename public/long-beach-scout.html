Long Beach Offense

How They Attack

Donaghy Is The Offense

#26 Cian Donaghy has 25 of the team's 110 points (23%). Add Conway's 19 goals and those two generate 38% of all offense. Take them out, the offense has to come from their middies (Farrell, Riedel, Hayes).

Offensive Profile

Two-Headed Attack: Donaghy + Conway

Donaghy (A, 14G/11A) is their primary initiator from attack — can feed or finish. Conway (A/M, 19G/0A) is their pure finisher. The give-and-go between these two is likely their go-to action.

Our counter: lock off Conway (force him to move without the ball). Put best pole on Donaghy. Make Brown or their middies create.

Middie Scoring Depth

Farrell (15 pts), Riedel (12 pts), Hayes (13 pts), Brown (14 pts). The offense has real depth — we can't just focus on the attackmen. Every dodge counts. Every slide must be on time.

46.7% assisted goals = mid-tier ball movement. The 2-man game and skip passes matter, but they can also beat you 1-on-1.

Weakness to Exploit

Charlie Conway has ZERO assists all season. He's a one-way player. If we can lock him off and force the ball to another player, we're winning the possession battle. Conway in a wall-ball set = not a playmaker.

Scoring Patterns by Quarter

Q1-Q2: Dormant (31 goals in 9 games = 3.4/half)

They score 3-4 goals per 1st half. If we're not up 3+ at halftime, something is wrong with us.

Q3: The Explosion (26 goals in 9 games = 2.9/Q3)

This is where they make games. Halftime adjustments, fresh legs, momentum — something clicks. Our Q3 defensive plan has to be identical to Q1 — same pressure, same slides, no letting up because we have a lead.

Long Beach Defense

How They Stop You

Starter Goalie Is Beatable

Aiden Derupo (Sr, starter) has a .523 save percentage. That's below average at varsity. He's giving up 47% of the shots he faces. If we get shots on cage, we score.

Bench watch: if Derupo gets pulled for #30 Jax Wagner (.694), tighten up immediately. Wagner is elite HS.

Defensive Profile

Conservative D — Not Pushing Transition

Their defenders recorded minimal offensive production all season. That's a signal: they're not pushing the ball up after saves. They play clear-first, then rely on set offense.

What it means for us: Our ride is where we can steal possessions. They don't have D-men who can beat a press with their stick.

They Give Up 9.8 Goals/Game

Their D gets picked apart when the pressure is on. In their losses they gave up 9, 14, 19, 13. Pressure makes this defense fall apart.

Goalie Game Plan

How to Beat Derupo (.523)
  • Shoot early, shoot often. First 3 minutes: get the ball on cage.
  • Don't overthink selection. Against .523, almost half goes in.
  • Test every angle — pipe shots, 5-hole, high-corner. Don't let him settle.
  • Crash every shot. He's likely giving up rebounds.
  • If Derupo stays hot, keep shooting. Variance favors us with 40+ shots.
If Wagner Comes In (.694) — Shift Strategy
  • Quality over quantity. No more bombs.
  • Inside finishes only — crease dumps, quick sticks, backdoor cuts
  • Screens + high-to-low deception
  • Fake high, shoot low. Change release points.

Clearing & Ride

Clear Target — Press Early

Defense is conservative → outlets are probably not elite stick handlers. Press their outlets in the 30-60 yard window. Force them to throw to their weak-hand side (tag on film).

Every Shot = Ride Opportunity

Hard ride after every shot. Their goalie Derupo gives up rebounds AND they don't push transition hard. Two extra possessions a game is probably the difference.

Man-Down (How They Defend EMO)

Unknown — Tag on Film

With a below-average starter goalie, Pearl may play zone to compress space. Or he may play man and rely on Wagner coming in if needed. Watch their EMD setup on film.

Our EMO: keep the ball hot, attack short-stick, backside hammer cuts, finish inside.

Plainedge Game Plan

How We Win

  1. Own the First 12 Minutes

    They get outscored 60-31 in 1st halves. If we're not up 3+ at halftime, we've underperformed. Set the tone on faceoff #1.

  2. Shoot Early & Often vs Derupo (.523)

    First 3 minutes: get shots on cage. He's giving up almost half. No hero shot selection — just get it on goal. Crash every shot for rebounds.

  3. Bench Watch — Alert for the Wagner Switch

    If Derupo struggles and Pearl pulls him for #30 Wagner (.694), we shift immediately to quality shots. Every coach on the bench watching that cage.

  4. Best Pole on #26 Donaghy

    Their most complete threat — 14G/11A, mixed finisher/feeder. Early-slide commitment. Deny feed lanes. Make him a 1-on-1 player.

  5. Lock Off #7 Conway

    19G, ZERO assists. He doesn't pass. Lock him off or deny him the ball. If we force him to pass, he's playing out of his game.

  6. Short-Stick Shutoff #9 Farrell

    Most productive middie (15 pts) with 7 assists — real feed threat from middie spot. Don't let him see the field. Low-poke, deny hands.

  7. Early Slide #6 Riedel

    Pure scoring middie (10G/2A). Downhill dodger. SSDM + early slide. Keep him lateral, no topside wins.

  8. Hunt the Sophs

    #3 Hayes (M), #16 Brown (A), #0 Connor Sullivan (LSM), #0 Buglisi (A/M) — any time one is on the field in a matchup we can attack, dodge immediately.

  9. Slide Early & Vertical

    Remove step-downs and middle-dodges. 2-slide live only to the crease. Everyone else rotates backside. No ball-watching.

  10. Ride Every Shot

    Their D is clear-first, not elite with sticks. Hard ride — attack clears at 30-60 yards, force weak-side outlets. Steal 2 extra possessions.

  11. Q3 Alert — They Flip Games

    LB is +14 in Q3 over the season. Halftime message: same pressure, same slides, no easing up. If they cut our lead in Q3, we lose the game.

  12. Faceoff — Exit First, Ball Second

    Deutsch (Sr) + Marra (Soph) split reps. No dominant FOGO. Win the exit, secure the second pass. On losses: immediate pressure, force bad first outlet.

  13. Man-Up: Donaghy Is The Feeder — Protect the Middle Seam

    Expect Donaghy to feed, Conway + a middie to shoot. Protect middle seam. Deny Donaghy's feed lanes and the EMO stalls.

Winning Score Target

Us 12, Them 6. Against Derupo's .523 we should score 11-13. Against their offense, if we shut down Donaghy + Conway + Farrell we hold them to 6-7.
