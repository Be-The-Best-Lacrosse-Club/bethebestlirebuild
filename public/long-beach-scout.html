Long Beach Offense

How They Attack

3-Initiator Offense

Cetrangolo + Myers produce the bulk. Diehl + Morra drive assists. Jennings is the downhill middie finisher. Expect 3 viable initiators — but only 2 primary feeders. Kill the feed and their assist-dependent offense stalls.

Offensive Profile

Balanced Scoring + Assist-Driven

46.7% assisted goals (75G / 35A). Mid-tier ball movement — not pure iso, not pure team offense. The 2-man game and skip passes matter.

Primary plan: Get Cetrangolo/Myers downhill, invert to matchup-hunt, then use Diehl/Morra as safety valves to reset and feed.

Weakness to Exploit

Offense leans on assisted goals. When their feeders are denied, shutoff, or forced into low-angle shots, they stall out. Our job is simple: choke the feed.

Scoring Patterns by Quarter

Q1-Q2: Dormant (31 goals in 9 games = 3.4/half)

They score 3-4 goals per 1st half. If we're not up 3+ at halftime, something is wrong with us.

Q3: The Explosion (26 goals in 9 games = 2.9/Q3)

This is where they make games. Halftime adjustments, fresh legs, momentum — something clicks. Our Q3 defensive plan has to be identical to Q1 — same pressure, same slides, no letting up because we have a lead.

Long Beach Defense

How They Stop You

The Goalie Is the Problem

Pepitone's .691 save percentage means they're allowing only 29 goals on 94 shots their defense lets through. Their D gives up a lot of shots — but the goalie eats the low-quality ones. Shot quality is the entire battle.

Defensive Profile

Conservative D — Not Pushing Transition

Their defenders recorded only 1 assist all season. That's a huge signal: they're not pushing the ball up after saves. They play clear-first, then rely on set offense.

What it means for us: Our ride is where we can steal possessions. They don't have D-men who can beat a press with their stick.

They Give Up 9.8 Goals/Game — But Stop the Bad Ones

Bottom line: if you get a low-angle alley shot, you're probably shooting into a save. High-percentage looks only. No hero shots.

Goalie — Matthew Pepitone (.691)

How to Beat Him
  • Inside finishes only. Picks, screens, crease dumps, quick sticks.
  • High-to-low deception. Fake high, shoot low. Change release points.
  • Make him move. Don't let him set — ball movement before shooting.
  • Screens. Make him track the shot through a body.
  • Get him late-quarter. Test if he fatigues.

Clearing & Ride

Clear Target — Press Early

Defense is conservative → outlets are probably not elite stick handlers. Press their outlets in the 30-60 yard window. Force them to throw to their weak-hand side (whichever that is — tag on film).

Every Shot = Ride Opportunity

Hard ride after every shot. Two extra possessions a game is probably the difference in this game.

Man-Down (How They Defend EMO)

Likely Zone-Heavy (Goalie-Confidence Driven)

A .691 goalie lets you play zone with more confidence. Expect a box or 1-3-2 zone with Pepitone as the backstop.

Our EMO answer: keep the ball hot, attack the short-stick, backside hammer cuts, finish from 8 yards.

Plainedge Game Plan

How We Win

  1. Own the First 12 Minutes

    They get outscored 60-31 in 1st halves. If we're not up 3+ at halftime we've underperformed. Set the tone on the first faceoff and don't let off.

  2. LSM on Myers

    Deny strong hand, chop wrists, force alley dodges. Myers is the initiator — make every possession a battle.

  3. Pole Cetrangolo

    Best pole on their top scorer. Ball must leave his stick without outlets. Make him create under pressure.

  4. Deny Diehl & Morra

    Short-stick shutoff on the feeders. Kill the feed and their assist-dependent offense stalls out. This is the single highest-leverage thing we can do.

  5. Jennings = Early Slide

    Pure scoring middie, no assists. Keep him lateral, deny topside, slide before the shot angle opens.

  6. Slide Early & Vertical — Not Horizontal

    Remove step-downs and middle dodges. 2-slide live only to the crease. Everyone else rotates backside. No ball-watching.

  7. Beat Pepitone With Shot Quality

    He's .691. Outside shots die. 6 great looks > 12 weak ones. Pick series for hands-free finishes, screens, quick sticks on the crease, high-to-low deception.

  8. Ride Every Shot

    Their D is clear-first, not elite with sticks. Hard ride — attack clears at 30-60 yards, force weak-side outlets. Steal 2 extra possessions.

  9. Q3 Alert — They Flip Games

    LB is +14 in Q3 over the season. Halftime message: same pressure, same slides, no easing up. If they cut our lead in Q3, we lose the game.

  10. Faceoff — Exit First, Ball Second

    FO reps are split (Gray, Chariton). No dominant FOGO. Win the exit, secure the second pass. On losses: immediate pressure, force a bad first outlet.

  11. Man-Up: Protect the Middle Seam

    Their EMO is multiple feeders → skip lanes and backside cuts are the threat. Protect the middle seam. Diehl/Morra are conduits — deny their touches.

Winning Score Target

Us 11, Them 7. Their floor is Manhasset (2 goals scored). Their ceiling is Mepham (11 scored). If we're disciplined and win Q1, we're closer to the floor than the ceiling.
