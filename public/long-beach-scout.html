Long Beach Offense

How They Attack

Controlled, High-Percentage Offense

Long Beach doesn't run-and-gun unless Derupo triggers it with a save + outlet. Their base action: isolate #2 Donaghy OR #3 Conway to draw the slide, then move the ball quickly to find the open man on the backside. They trust their feeders to deliver.

Primary Action — The 2-3 Game

Donaghy-Conway Give-and-Go

Per MacArthur film: Donaghy catches at X, waits for Conway to cut from the top, hits him in stride. This is their money play. Conway is the cutter, Donaghy is the passer.

Our read: if Donaghy is at X with the ball and Conway is ball-side up top, expect the feed. Backside defender can't ball-watch.

The "Either/Or" Trap

Manhasset shut Donaghy with a top pole (no slides off) → held LB to 2 goals. MacArthur sold out on Donaghy → Conway scored 5 including 2OT winner. You cannot "shut" both.

Our answer (MacArthur blueprint, executed correctly): Disciplined man with an LSM on Conway at the top of the box + a shutdown pole on Donaghy. Force Conway outside (sag) and Donaghy right (lefty). No jumping out of the system.

Transition — The Derupo → Miller Clear

Their Most Dangerous Weapon

Per Dan's film notes: "The Derupo-to-Miller clear bypasses the midfield and puts immediate pressure on the opponent's defense." Derupo saves → 40-yard outlet to Miller (#4) on the wing → immediate 4v3.

Our counter: After every shot, ride hard. Body up Miller's wing. Deny the outlet. Force Derupo to clear through midfield, not around it.

Second-Half Team

Long Beach increases tempo in Q3/Q4. They score 43 of 75 in the 2nd half. If we're in a toss-up at halftime, they're going to push. Win the early game or prepare for late adjustments.

Scoring Patterns by Quarter

Q1-Q2: Dormant (31 goals in 9 games = 3.4/half)

They score 3-4 goals per 1st half. If we're not up 3+ at halftime, something is wrong with us.

Q3: The Explosion (26 goals in 9 games = 2.9/Q3)

This is where they make games. Q3 defense = same as Q1. No lead is safe if we let off.

Long Beach Defense

How They Stop You

Starter Goalie Is Beatable

Aiden Derupo (Sr, starter) has a .523 save percentage. That's below average at varsity. He's giving up 47% of the shots he faces. If we get shots on cage, we score.

Bench watch: if Derupo gets pulled for #30 Jax Wagner (.694), tighten up immediately. Wagner is elite HS.

Defensive Profile

Conservative D — Not Pushing Transition

Their defenders recorded minimal offensive production all season. That's a signal: they're not pushing the ball up after saves. They play clear-first, then rely on set offense.

What it means for us: Our ride is where we can steal possessions. They don't have D-men who can beat a press with their stick.

They Give Up 9.8 Goals/Game

Their D gets picked apart when the pressure is on. In their losses they gave up 9, 14, 19, 13. Pressure makes this defense fall apart.

The "Derupo Problem" — Shot Plane Is Everything

Derupo's Strengths — DO NOT SHOOT HERE
  • Low-to-low shots (his specialty — he eats pipes)
  • Bounce shots (also his strength — NOT our exploit)
  • Shots at the shins = a free clear for LB
Derupo's Weaknesses — SHOOT HERE
  • Top-corner rips (high-to-high)
  • Off-hip shots
  • Lateral movement — skip passes across the crease make him slide; he's most vulnerable in motion
  • Per Carey tape: 8 saves on 18 shots = .444 that game — he IS beatable with the right planes
If Wagner (.694) Rotates In

Same shot-plane rule, tighter selection. Wagner played 6 saves in the MacArthur game alongside Derupo — they rotate deliberately. When #30 is in, emphasize picks, screens, and crease dumps over outside shots.

Clearing & Ride

Clear Target — Press Early

Defense is conservative → outlets are probably not elite stick handlers. Press their outlets in the 30-60 yard window. Force them to throw to their weak-hand side (tag on film).

Every Shot = Ride Opportunity

Hard ride after every shot. Their goalie Derupo gives up rebounds AND they don't push transition hard. Two extra possessions a game is probably the difference.

Man-Down (How They Defend EMO)

Unknown — Tag on Film

Our EMO: keep the ball hot, attack short-stick, backside hammer cuts, finish inside.

Plainedge Game Plan

How We Win

The MacArthur Blueprint (Did Not Quite Win — But Tied It)

MacArthur trailed 3-0 in Q1, forced 2OT by (1) high-pressure jumping Donaghy at X to force turnovers, (2) diversified scoring (6 different scorers, McDermott+McGee mirror-game), (3) shading Conway LEFT with an LSM at top of box, (4) long outlets over the top vs LB's 10-man ride, and (5) patience. They shut out LB in Q4.

We steal their blueprint and finish what they started.

Defensive Game Plan

  1. Shutdown Pole + No Slides on #2 Donaghy

    Top pole on Donaghy. Force him to his RIGHT (he's a lefty). Early slide commitment. No slides off him onto other threats. Manhasset held him in check and held LB to 2 goals.

  2. LSM on #3 Conway — Sag, Don't Press

    MacArthur's in-game fix: LSM at the top of the box (not a short-stick), shade Conway LEFT (his weak side). Do NOT press him — he rolls off contact. Sag and force outside shots.

  3. Don't Ball-Watch — Mirror Off-Ball

    LB's offense is iso → skip backside. Backside defender cannot watch the dodger. When a dodger moves, off-ball D holds shape. When a cutter moves, we go with him.

  4. Ride Every Shot — Especially After Derupo Saves

    The Derupo → Miller (#4) outlet is their most dangerous weapon. Sprint every ride. Body Miller on the wing. Force the clear through midfield, not around it.

  5. Wing Pressure on Parker's FO Exits

    Parker is a technical scrapper — wins by directed exits. Miller and Riedel are his primary wings. Clamp down the exit lanes. On our losses, pressure the outlet, not the clamp.

  6. Q3 Alert — They Flip Games

    LB is +14 in Q3 season-wide. And they don't panic — MacArthur film showed they stayed disciplined into 2OT. Same defensive pressure in Q3 as in Q1.

Offensive Game Plan

  1. No Low Shots. No Bounce Shots. EVER.

    Derupo eats low-to-low and bounce. Top-corner rips, high-to-high, off-hip only. Pre-game shooting brief: if you shoot at his shins, you just cleared his ride.

  2. Skip the Ball — Make Him Move Laterally

    Draw a slide one side, skip immediately to the opposite wing. Per MacArthur Q4: Derupo is most vulnerable when sliding across the crease.

  3. Attack Short-Stick at the Point — Avoid #4 Miller

    Miller is Hamilton-bound — don't dodge at him. Find the short-stick at the top of the box and drive. Forces their poles to slide inward, opens the crease for cutters.

  4. Push Tempo — Don't Settle

    Massapequa beat them 19-10 by running. LB is slow when the game is a track meet. If we win the faceoff, go. If we get a turnover, go. No "pull it out and set."

  5. Hang an Attackman on the 10-Man Ride

    Derupo comes out to pressure clears. Keep one attackman at the midline. Interception or long GB = look for the empty-net homerun pass or long-distance shot.

  6. Mirror Off-Ball — Force Ball-Watchers to Lose Their Man

    Per Carey film: LB defenders ball-watch. When our dodger moves right, our crease man moves left. Creates a split-second of confusion. Doorstep goals.

  7. Hunt Sophomore Matchups

    Connor Sullivan (Soph LSM), Dylan Brown (Soph A), Hudson Brenke (Frosh D), John Buglisi (Frosh A/M). When any are on, dodge that matchup immediately.

Faceoff & Transition

  1. FO Plan — Clamp or Counter Parker's Exit

    Parker directs exits to Miller/Riedel wings. Even on losses, make him work. On our wins, push fast-break before LB settles.

  2. Don't Let This Game Drift to OT

    MacArthur tape: LB is clutch in OT — disciplined 6v6 sets, Conway ice cold on the Golden Goal. Win this game in regulation. Don't let it stay close into Q4.

Winning Score Target

Us 12, Them 7. Execute the MacArthur blueprint + the correct shot plane + tempo pressure → we hit this target.
