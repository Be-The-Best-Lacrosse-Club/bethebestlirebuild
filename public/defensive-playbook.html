"The Standard Never Changes"
01

Defense Is Communication

Great defense isn't about one lockdown defender. It's about six guys talking, sliding, recovering, and rotating as one unit. Every concept in this playbook starts with your voice — if you're not talking, you're not playing defense.

Plainedge Communication System

Every defender on the field must be talking at all times. These are the calls your teammates need to hear:

  • "BALL! BALL! BALL!"I am guarding the ball carrier. Everyone else adjusts to me.
  • "ONE!"I am one pass away. I'm in help position, ready to slide.
  • "TWO!"I am two passes away. I'm in the hole, splitting the difference.
  • "HOT!"I am the first slide if the on-ball defender gets beat.
  • "TWO-SLIDE!"I am the second slide, covering whoever the hot man left.
  • "PICK LEFT/RIGHT!"I see a screen coming. Telling my teammate which side it's on.
  • "SWITCH!"We are swapping assignments on this pick.
  • "GET THROUGH!"Fight over the pick, I'll hedge and you recover.
  • "CUTTER!"Off-ball attacker is moving through the lane. Someone bump him.
  • "CHECK!"Count your man. Everyone should have one.
  • "JAM!"Jump and double the two-man pick game. Both defenders attack the ball carrier simultaneously — blow the play up before it develops.
  • "ACTIVE. ACTIVE!"Get those sticks up and cause chaos. High hands, disrupt passing lanes, make every throw uncomfortable. Force the mistake.
  • "AJ GO!"Adjacent slide — and we are going sooner rather than later. AJ commits NOW. Don't wait for the full dodge. Pre-slide on the first step of the ball carrier.
  • "AJs. TIME TO PARTY!"Hard double and lock on AJ's man. Our goalie finds the furthest open guy immediately and outlets. Everyone else finds their man — no standing around.

Key Defensive Terms

Adjacent Slide
The defender NEXT TO the on-ball defender is the first slide. Creates short, fast rotations.
Crease Slide
The defender on the crease player is the first slide. Longer distance but protects the middle.
Near Man
Combination of both — the nearest defender slides regardless of position. Most instinctive, hardest to scout.
Hedge
Step out to slow the ball carrier, then recover to your man. You're buying time, not switching.
Recovery
After the slide is made and the ball is passed, sprinting back to find an open man and picking him up.
Bump
When a cutter runs through your zone, you check him and redirect him — then pass him to the next defender.
Split
When you're two-away, you position between two attackers so you can cover whichever one gets the ball.
02

Slides & Recovery

The slide is everything. When the on-ball defender gets beat, the hot man slides. When the hot man slides, the two-slide rotates. When the ball moves, everyone recovers. This is the heartbeat of team defense — and it starts with recognizing who's hot before the dodge happens.

POWLAXMen's D1
Adjacent Slide Basics

🎯 Key Focus

The adjacent slide means the defender NEXT to the on-ball guy is the first slide — fastest because the distance is shortest. Watch how the adjacent defender pre-positions before the dodge even starts.

👀 Watch For

Pistol stance: one foot pointed at his man, body angled toward the ball. Two hard steps when the dodge starts — decisive, not hesitant.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Waiting to see the full dodge before committing. Those two steps must be immediate.

POWLAXMen's D1
Crease Slide Basics

🎯 Key Focus

The crease slide means the defender guarding the crease attackman is the first slide. Longer distance, but protects the most dangerous area on the field.

👀 Watch For

The two-slide is CRITICAL here — crease is left open. If the two-slide is late, the offense dumps it to an unguarded crease man for a tap-in. The secondary slide must be automatic.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Two-slide being a step behind. The rotation must be initiated as soon as the crease slide commits — not after.

POWLAXMen's D1
Near Man Defense — The Complete System

🎯 Key Focus

Near Man combines adjacent + crease into one instinctive system: the NEAREST defender slides, regardless of position. One rule — react to proximity.

👀 Watch For

Plainedge runs Near Man because it's the most instinctive. No memorizing packages — just follow one rule. Recovery happens from the outside in after the slide.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Overthinking who is "supposed to" slide. If you're closest — you go. Period.

POWLAXDrill
Salisbury Call Drill — Slide & Recover

🎯 Key Focus

Fast-paced drill that builds sliding, recovering, bumping, and playing the two inside. This is the best drill for building slide-and-recover instincts because the reps come fast.

👀 Watch For

This drill builds the RECOVERY habit. After you slide and the ball moves, you must SPRINT to find an open man. You can't jog back — the offense is already swinging it.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Jogging back after the slide. Every rep must end with a full sprint to recovery position.

The Secondary Slide — Why It Matters Most: The first slide stops the dodger. The SECONDARY SLIDE stops the goal. When the hot man leaves, the two-slide must rotate to that open man BEFORE the pass arrives. Teach your players: "When the hot man goes, I rotate IMMEDIATELY — I don't wait to see what happens."

Plainedge AJ Call System
"AJ GO!"
Adjacent Slide — Early & Aggressive

AJ = Adjacent Slide. "GO" means we are going sooner rather than later — pre-slide on the first step of the dodger, don't wait for the on-ball defender to get beat. Commit early, make the dodger decide before he's in open space.

Key Rule: AJ reads the first body lean of the ball carrier — that's the trigger. Don't watch the feet, watch the hips.
"AJs. TIME TO PARTY!"
Hard Double + Lock + Goalie Outlet

AJ goes hard double and locks his man — full commit, no soft hedge. The moment the double is on, our goalie reads the field and outlets to the furthest open guy. Everyone else is already finding their man before the pass arrives. No standing, no waiting.

Key Rule: Goalie does NOT look for the closest guy — he finds the furthest. Stretch the field. Turn pressure into a fast break opportunity.
03

Defending Picks & 2-Man Games

Every D1 offense runs 2-man games. Picks, screens, give-and-goes — the defense has three options: let your man fight through, switch, or hedge and recover. The choice depends on where the pick is, who's involved, and what the offense is trying to create. Communication is EVERYTHING.

POWLAXMen's D1
Defending 2-Man Games — Full Breakdown

🎯 Key Focus

Covers every type of 2-man game: picks, shallow cuts, mirrors, fades, give-and-goes, overlaps. Each has a different defensive solution.

👀 Watch For

The defender guarding the PICKER must communicate FIRST — he sees the pick coming before his teammate. Call "PICK LEFT" or "PICK RIGHT" early and loud, then call the response.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Nobody talks. If nobody talks, the pick works every single time.

POWLAXMen's D1Youth-Friendly
Executing & Defending 2-Man Games — Master Class

🎯 Key Focus

Complete master class from BOTH sides of the ball — perfect pick, pick-and-roll options, and how the defense responds to each one.

👀 Watch For

Study the defensive response sections specifically. Understanding what the offense is trying to do helps you anticipate it before it happens.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Treating every pick the same. Location and personnel change the right response every time.

POWLAXMen's D1
2-Man Game — Pick Locations

🎯 Key Focus

Where the pick is set changes how you defend it. A pick up top is different from X. A pick on the wing is different from the crease.

👀 Watch For

Near the cage → SWITCH. Can't afford a free lane. Far from cage → GET THROUGH. Time to recover. Wing/alley → HEDGE. Slow the dodger, then recover. Predictable set play → JAM. Attack it before it develops. Settled offense → ACTIVE. ACTIVE. Sticks up, cause chaos, force the mistake.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Using the same response for every pick regardless of location. Teach the rule, not the exception.

Five Ways to Defend a Pick

  1. GET THROUGH (Fight): On-ball defender fights over or under the screen. Use when the ball is far from the cage — no immediate shooting threat.
  2. SWITCH: Defenders swap assignments at the pick. Simple and fast, but can create mismatches. Call "SWITCH!" early so both defenders hear it. Use near the cage when you need to stay tight.
  3. HEDGE & RECOVER: Pick defender steps out to slow the ball carrier — buying his teammate time — then sprints back. Requires the most athleticism and communication. Call "HEDGE!" so the on-ball defender knows help is coming.
  4. JAM — Jump & Double: Both the on-ball defender AND the pick defender attack the ball carrier at the same time. Blow the 2-man game up before it can develop. Best used when the pick is set in a predictable spot and you want to send a message early in the game. High risk — remaining four defenders must rotate hard to cover open men.
  5. ACTIVE. ACTIVE — Stick Chaos: No single man doubles — instead every defender elevates their sticks into passing lanes simultaneously. You're not going for the steal, you're making every pass uncomfortable and forcing bad decisions. Call it when the offense is settled and swinging — disrupt their rhythm before they find a crease feed.
04

Defending Cutters & Help

Off-ball defense is where youth teams fall apart. You MUST see ball and man at all times. When a cutter runs through your zone, bump him and redirect. When the offense clears through, help by doubling, then recover when the ball moves.

POWLAXYouth-Friendly
Basics of Individual Defensive Play

🎯 Key Focus

The triangle: Ball → You → Your Man. Always able to point one hand at the ball and one at your man. Off-ball defense is about POSITIONING, not chasing.

👀 Watch For

Foot placement, body angle, and maintaining ball-you-man triangle so you see both the ball AND your man simultaneously.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Watching only the ball and losing sight of your man — or watching only your man and losing the ball. You must see both.

POWLAXDrillYouth-Friendly
Building Defense with 3v0s, 3v2s, and 3v3s

🎯 Key Focus

Part-whole method: start 3v0 (motion only), add 3v2 (help defense, no on-ball), then 3v3 (full). Builds defensive habits progressively before 6v6.

👀 Watch For

The 3v2 is the magic drill for learning help defense. Two defenders guard three — you MUST communicate, split, and help on cutters. No hiding.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Jumping straight to 6v6 before players understand help positioning. Build it in the 3v2 first.

The Bump System — Defending Cutters

  • See the cutter EARLY. If your head is buried on the ball, a cutter will run through your zone uncovered. Head on a swivel.
  • Call "CUTTER!" so the whole defense knows someone is moving through the lane.
  • BUMP the cutter — step into his path, make legal body contact, redirect his route. Don't chase him — redirect him.
  • PASS the cutter to the next defender. You bump, the next man picks him up. It's a relay, not a man-to-man track meet.
  • After the cutter passes through, recover to your original position and find the ball.

Doubling on a Clear-Through: When an offensive player clears through behind the cage, the defense has a brief numerical advantage. The two-away defender should jump into the passing lane and provide heavy help. When the ball moves, he recovers back. The clear-through is a GIFT to the defense — use it.

05

Man-Down & Disadvantage

When you're down a man — penalty or live-play disadvantage — the defense must rotate as a unit. You're outnumbered. Someone is open. Your job is to make the offense work for a BAD shot instead of an easy one.

POWLAXMen's D1Youth-Friendly
Box and 1 — Man-Down Foundation

🎯 Key Focus

Four defenders form a box, one plays the ball. When the ball moves, the box rotates. Simple enough for any level — teaches fundamentals of man-down rotation.

👀 Watch For

In man-down, you can't guard everyone. Accept it. Guard the INSIDE — take away the crease, take away the easy feed, force distance shots.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Chasing the ball carrier on the perimeter and leaving the crease wide open. Sprint inside first.

POWLAXMen's D1 Film
House Zone — Virginia's Championship MDD

🎯 Key Focus

Virginia's House Zone (2019 NCAA Tournament) creates confusion because the rotation pattern differs from a standard box. Everyone moves together or it breaks.

👀 Watch For

Built on one principle: rotate to the ball, recover away from the ball. As the ball swings, defense rotates one position toward it. Ball reverses — defense goes opposite. It's a clock.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

One defender not rotating with the group. The House Zone only works if all five move together every time.

POWLAXAdvanced
Pushin' P — Pressure Man-Down Defense

🎯 Key Focus

Most MDD is passive — protect the inside. Pushin' P flips it: apply pressure on the ball, force turnovers, stay aggressive even when outnumbered. High risk, high reward.

👀 Watch For

For advanced teams only — after mastering Box and 1. Don't pressure man-down if your players don't yet understand rotation. Learn the foundation first, then add pressure.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Running this before your team is ready. Pushin' P before Box and 1 is a recipe for goals against.

Live-Play Disadvantage — Same Principles

  • Sprint to the hole. Get inside, protect the crease. Don't chase the ball carrier on the perimeter.
  • Communicate who has the ball. One defender picks up the dodger. Everyone else rotates inside.
  • Buy time. Every second you delay, a teammate is sprinting back to even the numbers.
  • Force the outside shot. Take away the crease feed. Make them shoot from distance.
06

3-3 Zone Defense

The 3-3 zone splits the field into six zones — three up top, three down low. Defenders guard zones, not men. Advantage: covers the middle and takes away inside feeds. Weakness: can be beaten by quick ball movement and skip passes. Communication holds it together.

POWLAXD3 ChampionshipYouth-Friendly
Wesleyan's Championship 3-3 Zone

🎯 Key Focus

Zone defense is about TEAM MOVEMENT, not individual matchups. When the ball moves, ALL SIX defenders shift together — like a blanket. If one doesn't shift, a gap opens.

👀 Watch For

Top three shift with the ball — ball goes right, all three shift right. Bottom three protect the crease. The shift must be simultaneous, not sequential.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

One defender shifting late and leaving a seam. Zone only works when all six move at the same time.

POWLAXAdvanced
Wild Cat Trap Zone Defense

🎯 Key Focus

Takes the 3-3 formation but flips the principles — instead of sitting back, you TRAP the ball carrier. Top three create a double-team, bottom three cover passing lanes.

👀 Watch For

High risk, high reward. Works when your team has athletic defenders who can cover ground. Master the standard 3-3 first — use this as a tournament changeup.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Running the trap before your team understands the standard zone. You'll give up wide-open shots every time.

Zone Communication Calls

  • "ZONE! ZONE!"Signal to the entire defense that we're switching from man to zone.
  • "SHIFT LEFT/RIGHT!"Ball has moved. Everyone shifts as a unit.
  • "CUTTER THROUGH!"Attacker cutting through your zone. Bump and pass him to next zone.
  • "SKIP! SKIP!"Cross-field pass coming. Weak-side top defender must jump out.
  • "LOCK!"Switch from zone back to man-to-man on a specific player.
07

Film Session Plan

Progress from individual positioning → slide systems → pick defense → man-down → zone. Each session is one practice film block. Don't skip ahead — every layer builds on the previous one.

S1

Individual D & Communication

15 min
Foundation: Body Position & Voice

Individual Defensive Play. Teach ball-you-man triangle. Then review the full communication system — every player must know the calls before they can play team defense.

S2

Slides & Recovery

20 min
Adjacent, Crease, and Near Man

Adjacent → Crease → Near Man → Salisbury Call Drill. Drill the AJ call system: "AJ GO!" for early commitment and "AJs. TIME TO PARTY!" for hard double + goalie outlet to furthest man. Recovery is just as important as the initial slide.

S3

Defending Picks & 2-Man Games

15 min
Communication Through Screens

Defending 2-Man Games → Pick Locations. Teach the three responses: Get Through, Switch, Hedge. Drill the call system — picker calls it first, every time.

S4

Cutters, Help, and Doubling

15 min
Off-Ball Awareness

Building O&D with 3v0s/3v2s/3v3s. Teach the bump system for cutters and the clear-through help principle. Use the 3v2 drill in practice immediately after film.

S5

Man-Down Rotation

15 min
Surviving the Disadvantage

Box and 1 → House Zone. Teach Box and 1 first — full understanding before adding the House Zone as a second look. Rotation speed determines if you survive.

S6

3-3 Zone Defense

15 min
Zone Principles & The Trap

Wesleyan 3-3 → Wild Cat Trap. Standard zone first — shift as a unit, cover zones not men. Add the trap as an advanced tournament changeup.

S7

Pressure Man-Down

10 min
Advanced: Aggressive MDD

Pushin' P. Only show after Sessions 1–5 are mastered. For teams ready to apply pressure in disadvantage situations. Use as a tournament changeup — never a base defense.

After Every Film Session — The Communication Test

Before players leave the film room, point at a random player and ask:

"You're the adjacent defender. Your teammate gets beat on an alley dodge. What do you say? What do you do?"

Every player should be able to answer instantly. If they hesitate, they're not ready. Run it again.
